# Container

Base for most pages, containers define the content's bounds.

## How to use

To define a container, you must only attribute the class `lx-container-$`, where `$` is the percentage of the page's width that you wish it to span, as in the example below:

```html
<div class="lx-container-80">
  <div>
    Your content here
  </div>
</div>
```

<div className="lx-container-80" style={{
  marginBottom: "1em",
  padding: "1em 1em .1em 1em",
  backgroundColor: "rgb(31,31,31)"
}}>
  <div>
    Your content here
  </div>
</div>

The lower limit for a container is 50% of its parent's width.

```html Lower-limit
<div class="lx-container-50">
  <div>
    Your content here
  </div>
</div>
```

<div className="lx-container-50" style={{
  marginBottom: "1em",
  padding: "1em 1em .1em 1em",
  backgroundColor: "rgb(31,31,31)"
}}>
  <div>
    Your content here
  </div>
</div>

From this point upward, you can define containers with width percentages in increments of 5.

```html Increments-of-5
<div class="lx-container-55">
  <div>
    Your content here
  </div>
</div>
```

<div className="lx-container-55" style={{
  marginBottom: "1em",
  padding: "1em 1em .1em 1em",
  backgroundColor: "rgb(31,31,31)"
}}>
  <div>
    Your content here
  </div>
</div>

Other percentages are considered invalid.

```html Invalid-percentage
<div class="lx-container-66">
  <div>
    Your content here
  </div>
</div>
```

<div className="lx-container-66" style={{
  marginBottom: "1em",
  padding: "1em 1em .1em 1em",
  backgroundColor: "rgb(31,31,31)"
}}>
  <div>
    Your content here
  </div>
</div>

The upper limit is 100% of its parent's width.

```html Upper-limit

    Your content here
```
